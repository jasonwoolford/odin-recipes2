Chef John's Chicken Tortilla Soup

Sourced from allrecipes.com

Description

A delicious, savory soup made from a quality chicken broth, hearty shredded chicken, and robust tomatoes. Tortillas can be store bought or made fresh at home!

Core Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, 4.5 lbs in weight
  • 3 white onions, halved
  • 1 bunch of cilantro, rinsed well
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon Mexican oregano
  • 2 dried chilli pepper pods, seeded
  • 3 quarts water

Tortilla Strips

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt

Charred Vegetable Salsa

  • 3 large tomatoes
  • 1 sweet red pepper, halved and seeded
  • 6 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 whole canned chipotle pepper in adobo sauce (optional)
    Finishings:
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 lime, juiced
    Garnishes:
  • Sour cream
  • Sliced avocado
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Shredded or crumbled Mexican cheese
  • Lime wedges
  • Sprikle of cayenne pepper

Directions

  1. Combine whole chicken, 1 onion, cilantro stems, bay leaves, oregano, peppercorns, dried chili pods, and 3 quarts in a large soup pot on medium-high heat. Bring to simmer, then lower heat to medium-low, let simmer for 1 hour.

    2. Tortilla strip instructions:

    1. If making tortilla strips at home: Preheat an oven to 350°F/180°C, and line a baking sheet with parchment paper, or a silicone mat if preferable.
    2. Cut tortillas in half, then crosswise in 1/4" strips; transfer tortilla strips to the prepared baking sheet and drizzle with oil. Add salt and toss well to coat, then spread strips out evenly.
    3. Bake until crispy or golden brown, tossing once or twice during the 30 minute cook time.

  2. With caution, remove chicken from the simmering pot, and place it into a seperate bowl. Reduce the heat on the simmering broth to low.
  3. Let chicken cool so that the meat can be safely pulled away from the chicken, shred and refridgerate the chicken until needed.
  4. Add chicken bones, skin, and scraps to the broth, simmer on low heat for an additional 4 hours. Water may be added to broth if it reduces too much during this time.

    5. Salsa instructions:

    1. After two hours of further simmering the broth, preheat the oven's broiler for high heat and set a rack about 8 inches below the heating element.
    2. Line a baking sheet with foil, and oil lightly. Place two onions, tomatoes, sweet red pepper, and jalapeno pepper onto sheet.
    3. Broil vegetables until charred, 7 to 10 minutes. Add charred vegetables to a blender with garlic cloves, chipotle pepper, and 1 cup water, blend until smooth
    4. Pour salsa puree into the pot, raise heat to medium-low. Simmer broth for another 2 hours, stirring occasionally.

  5. Following the four hours of simmering, pass soup through a fine-mesh strainer into a seperate pot, squeezing the chicken to strain chicken juices into the new pot; discard bones and skin in strainer, and season the soup to taste.
  6. Add cumin, shredded chicken and juice from 1 lime. Simmer 15 to 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste for seasoning, and serve with crispy tortilla strips. Garnish with sour cream, avocado, cilantro, Mexican cheese, additional lime juice, and a dash of cayenne, if desired.
