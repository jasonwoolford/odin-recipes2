Slow Cooker Texas Pulled Pork

sourced from allrecipes.com

Description

A simple texas-style pulled pork cookes in barbeque sauce, made easily in a slow cooker. Excellent on toasted butter rolls!

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 4 pound pork shoulder roast
  • 1 cup barbeque sauce
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon prepared yellow mustard
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 extra large onion, chopped
  • 2 large cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 large cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 8 hamburger buns, split
  • 2 tablespoons butter, or as needed

Directions

  1. Pour vegetable oil into the bottom of a slow cooker, followed by the pork roast.
  2. Stir in brown sugar, yellow mustard, Worcestershire sauce, chili powder, onion, garlic, and thyme. Cover and cook on low for 10 to 12 hours, or high for 5 to 6 hours, until the pork shreds easily with a fork.
  3. After letting the pork cook, remove from the slow cooker and shred using two forks. Return the shredded pork to the slow cooker and stir to combine with the rest of the ingredients.
  4. Spread butter on the insides of both halves of your hamburger buns, then toast the buns butter-side down in a skillet on medium heat until golden brown. Spoon pulled pork onto toasted buns.
